Best Streaming Apps Without a Subscription (2026)

By the ViMovies Team · Updated July 6, 2026

The average streaming household now pays for four subscriptions. Prices rise yearly, password sharing is locked down, and content jumps between services. There's a growing counter-movement: streaming apps that require no subscription at all. No trials that convert to charges. No credit card fields. We tested the genuinely free options — here are the seven worth installing in 2026.

1. ViMovies — No Subscription, No Account, No Catch

ViMovies is the purest expression of subscription-free streaming: install it, open it, watch. There is no registration wall, and features that paid services gate — offline downloads, 4K playback, automatic subtitles — are simply included. It runs on Android TV, Google TV, Fire TV, and Android phones. Our installation guide gets you running in five minutes.

2. Tubi — The Licensed Giant

Tubi proves the ad-supported model can work at scale: a massive, fully licensed catalog funded by ad breaks shorter than broadcast TV. An account is optional and only needed for cross-device resume.

3. Pluto TV — Lean-Back Live TV

Hundreds of free live channels organized by theme — movies, comedy, classic series — plus an on-demand section. Nothing to configure, nothing to pay.

4. Plex Free Tier — Streaming Meets Your Own Library

Plex's free ad-supported catalog is substantial, and if you own any media files, the app organizes them alongside it. Requires a free account.

5–7. Roku Channel, Kanopy, Crackle

  • The Roku Channel works well beyond Roku hardware through its mobile and web apps.
  • Kanopy is a hidden gem: free with a public library card, zero ads, and an outstanding documentary and arthouse selection.
  • Crackle maintains a smaller rotating catalog with strong classic picks.

How to Build a Zero-Subscription Setup

  1. Install ViMovies as your primary movie app on TV and mobile.
  2. Add Tubi for licensed catalog depth.
  3. Add Pluto TV for live channel surfing.
  4. Check Kanopy if you hold a library card — it's the only ad-free option here.
