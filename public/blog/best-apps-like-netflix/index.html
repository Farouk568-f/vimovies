Best Free Apps Like Netflix in 2026

By the ViMovies Team · Updated July 6, 2026

Netflix's cheapest ad-free plan has nearly doubled in five years. If you're canceling — or just trimming — you don't have to give up the Netflix experience: the browsable rows, the resume-watching, the it-just-works playback. These eight free apps replicate that experience surprisingly well in 2026.

What Makes an App “Like Netflix”?

  • A rich browsing interface with curated rows and artwork
  • Instant playback with resume across sessions
  • A deep catalog of both movies and series
  • Great apps on TV and mobile

1. ViMovies — The Closest Feel to Netflix on a TV

Open ViMovies on an Android TV and the resemblance is immediate: horizontal rows of artwork, a hero banner, smooth focus animations, and one-click resume. Unlike Netflix, there's no monthly fee, no profiles wall, and it adds offline downloads on every platform free of charge. See why it scored 8.8/10 in our full review, then follow the install guide.

2. Tubi — The Catalog Heavyweight

Tubi's interface borrows heavily from Netflix's playbook, and its licensed catalog is one of the largest in free streaming. Expect ad breaks, but shorter than cable.

3. Plex — Best If You Own Any Media

Plex wraps your personal library and its free streaming catalog in one Netflix-style interface. The discover section unifies search across services — genuinely useful when content is scattered.

4. Pluto TV — For Channel Surfers

Less Netflix, more classic cable — but the on-demand section has grown into a respectable library, and the price is right.

5–8. Roku Channel, Crackle, Kanopy, Stremio

The Roku Channel and Crackle are solid secondary installs with rotating catalogs. Kanopy is the quality pick — ad-free with a library card. Stremio offers the deepest series tracking if you don't mind configuring add-ons (see our comparison).

The Cancel-Netflix Playbook

  1. Install ViMovies on your TV and phone — it covers the core Netflix experience.
  2. Add Tubi for licensed depth and Pluto TV for live channels.
  3. Keep a running watchlist; if a must-see original appears on Netflix, subscribe for one month, binge, and cancel again.

Most households following this playbook save $200+ per year. More zero-cost options are in our guide to streaming apps without a subscription.

Frequently Asked Questions

What free app is most similar to Netflix?

ViMovies comes closest in feel: a polished row-based interface, autoplay previews of details, resume watching, and profiles-free instant access — without the monthly fee.

Can free apps replace Netflix completely?

For many households, yes. Combining ViMovies, Tubi, and Pluto TV covers movies, series, and live TV. You lose Netflix originals but keep a vast catalog at zero cost.

Do these alternatives work on smart TVs?

All the apps listed support Android TV and Google TV. Most also run on Fire TV, Roku, and mobile devices.
