The 10 Best Free Movie Apps in 2026

By the ViMovies Team · Updated July 6, 2026

Streaming subscriptions now cost more than cable did a decade ago. The good news: in 2026 there are more quality free movie apps than ever. We installed and tested the leading options on Android TV, Google TV, and mobile devices, judging each on catalog, interface, ads, and extra features.

1. ViMovies — Best TV-First Free Movie App

ViMovies tops our list because it nails the fundamentals other free apps miss: a genuinely remote-friendly interface, fast 4K playback, automatic subtitles, and built-in offline downloads — a feature that even paid services often restrict. There's no account requirement and no subscription. Read our full ViMovies review or jump straight to the installation guide.

2. Tubi — Largest Free Licensed Catalog

Fox-owned Tubi offers tens of thousands of licensed movies and shows, fully free with ads. The catalog rotates monthly and includes a surprising number of recent, recognizable titles. Ad breaks are shorter than live TV.

3. Pluto TV — Best for Live Channels

Pluto recreates the channel-surfing experience with hundreds of themed live channels plus an on-demand library. Ideal for background viewing when you don't want to choose.

4. Plex — Best Hybrid of Personal and Free Streaming

Plex began as a personal media server and now bundles a large free ad-supported catalog. If you own any media files, Plex is unmatched at organizing them alongside free streaming content.

5. Stremio — Best for Customization

Stremio is a hub you extend with add-ons. Setup takes effort, but the result is tailored to you. See our detailed ViMovies vs Stremio comparison.

6–10. The Rest of the Best

  • The Roku Channel — solid free catalog, works beyond Roku hardware via mobile apps.
  • Crackle — smaller library with a strong classic-movie selection.
  • Freevee content in Prime Video — Amazon folded its free tier into Prime Video; no Prime subscription needed for ad-supported titles.
  • Kanopy — free with a public library card; excellent documentaries and arthouse cinema, no ads.
  • Kodi — open-source media center for power users with local libraries.

How We Tested

Each app was installed on a Chromecast with Google TV and a mid-range Android phone. We evaluated catalog size and freshness, ad frequency, interface speed with a remote, subtitle support, and offline capability over one week of real use.

Bottom Line

You no longer need a stack of subscriptions to watch great movies. Start with ViMovies for the best TV experience and offline viewing, add Tubi for licensed depth, and Pluto TV for live channels. For Android TV-specific picks, see our guide to the best movie apps for Android TV.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best free movie app in 2026?

It depends on your priorities. ViMovies leads for TV-first design and offline downloads, Tubi has the largest licensed free catalog, and Plex is best if you also keep a personal media library.

Are free movie apps legal?

Ad-supported services like Tubi, Pluto TV, and Plex are fully licensed. For any app, only stream content that is legally available in your region.

Do free movie apps work on Android TV?

Most of the apps on this list have dedicated Android TV versions. ViMovies, Tubi, Pluto TV, and Plex all offer excellent big-screen interfaces.
