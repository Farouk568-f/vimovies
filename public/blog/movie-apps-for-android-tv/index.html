Best Movie Apps for Android TV: 2026 Rankings

By the ViMovies Team · Updated July 6, 2026

Android TV is the most open smart-TV platform, but that openness cuts both ways: the Play Store is full of movie apps that are really phone apps wearing a TV costume. Broken remote navigation, tiny touch targets, sluggish menus. We tested the field on real TV hardware — a Chromecast with Google TV and an entry-level TV box — to find the movie apps that genuinely belong on a television.

What We Judged

  • Remote navigation: can you reach everything with a D-pad, without a mouse?
  • Performance: does it stay smooth on 1.5–2 GB RAM TV boxes?
  • Catalog: depth, freshness, and 4K availability
  • Extras: subtitles, resume playback, downloads

1. ViMovies — The Benchmark for TV-First Design

ViMovies was clearly built for the living room before anything else. Rows glide, focus states are unmissable from the couch, and the back button always does what you expect. On our low-end test box it stayed responsive where heavier apps stuttered. Add 4K playback, automatic subtitles, and offline downloads, and it earns the top spot. 2. Tubi — Licensed Depth With a Great TV App

Tubi's Android TV app is one of the most polished free apps on the platform. The catalog is enormous and fully licensed, funded by moderate ad breaks. The only miss: no offline downloads on TV.

3. Plex — Your Media Plus Free Streaming

If you keep any personal media collection, Plex's Android TV app organizes it beautifully and layers a large free ad-supported catalog on top. Slightly heavier on low-end hardware than the top two.

4. Pluto TV — Channel Surfing Reinvented

Pluto's strength is live themed channels — great when you want TV to just play. On-demand selection is decent, and the TV app handles remotes well.

5. Stremio — For Users Who Like to Tinker

Powerful once configured with add-ons, but the setup process and desktop-first UI keep it out of the top tier for pure TV use. Full breakdown in ViMovies vs Stremio.

6. Kodi — The Open-Source Veteran

Kodi remains superb for local media libraries with a fully customizable interface. As a streaming app it depends entirely on what you add to it.

Tips for a Better Movie Experience on Android TV

  • Match frame rate: enable it in your TV box's display settings to eliminate judder in movies.
  • Wired beats wireless: if your box has Ethernet, use it for reliable 4K streaming.
  • Clear app caches monthly: low-storage TV devices slow down as caches grow.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes an app good for Android TV specifically?

Remote-control (D-pad) navigation, a 10-foot interface with large focus states, fast performance on low-RAM TV hardware, and proper leanback launcher integration.

Can I install apps on Android TV that aren't in the Play Store?

Yes. Android TV supports sideloading APKs through a browser or the Downloader app after enabling installation from unknown sources.

Do these apps work on Nvidia Shield?

Yes. The Nvidia Shield runs Android TV, so every app in this list works — and its extra power makes 4K playback especially smooth.
